Oasis is one of the most successful and influential rock bands to emerge from the UK in the 1990s. Formed in Manchester in 1991, the band was led by the Gallagher brothers and became pioneers of the Britpop movement.

Rise to Fame

Oasis burst onto the scene with their debut album *Definitely Maybe* (1994), the fastest-selling debut album in British history at the time. Their second album, *What's the Story) Morning Glory?* (1995), propelled them to global superstardom.

  • Definitely Maybe (1994)
  • (What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995)
  • Be Here Now (1997)

Legacy and Influence

Oasis' energy, catchy melodies, and rebellious attitude made them the voice of a generation. Despite disbanding in 2009, their music has stood the test of time and continues to inspire.

  • Formed: 1991 in Manchester, UK
  • Genres: Britpop, Rock
  • Notable Hits: "Wonderwall," "Champagne Supernova," "Live Forever"
